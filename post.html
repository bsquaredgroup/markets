Daily Report for Friday 14th April 2023

Posted by Ben Kelsey on April 14, 2023

U.S. economic data is finally showing some signs of softening, with lower Consumer Price Index (CPI), Producer Price Index (PPI), and increasing jobless claims. PPI fell by 0.5%, its largest drop since the COVID outbreak, indicating a slowdown in the services sector. Jobless claims rose by 11,000 to 239,000, continuing an upward trend.

Despite this data, U.S. Treasury yields didn't react, and the market is still pricing in an 18 basis point increase for a rate hike in May. Equities surged due to cooler than expected inflation data, and with plenty of cash available for investment, we can expect a strong end to the week. The U.S. dollar weakened as markets grew more comfortable with the possibility of the Federal Reserve pausing its tightening cycle and the peak of the dollar.

The risk-on sentiment has driven the New Zealand dollar (Kiwi) higher, as it was oversold and importers are now reentering the market. Exporters can look at 0.6220, while importers are targeting the 0.6300/20 range. The NZD/AUD pair has moved back into its usual range after another sell-off, with the 92-94 cent range holding firm. Fundamentals favor the Australian dollar, while interest rates support the Kiwi. Both the euro and British pound reached new yearly highs.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average rose by 383 points, the Nasdaq by 236 points, NZX by 13 points, and the ASX fell by 19 points. Oil prices stand at $82.28 per barrel, with carbon prices at $62.50 and Bitcoin at $30.2k.

New Zealand construction costs increased by 0.6% in Q1, bringing the annual rate down to 8.5% from 10.5%. The long-term Crane Index reached a record high of 157, although it remains to be seen if this level will be sustained. Overseas worker arrivals hit a new record in March at 20,442 people, higher than pre-COVID levels. As the construction sector faces challenges, we may experience a housing shortage.

Today, we'll see the release of New Zealand's Manufacturing PMI data, and U.S. banks, including JPMorgan, Wells Fargo, and Citigroup, will report their earnings.

Enjoy your weekend!
