Daily Report for Wednesday 26th April 2023

Posted by Ben Kelsey on April 26, 2023

Global markets experienced a not so pretty performance, with the US market under pressure, European markets mostly down, and Asian markets showing mixed results.

US markets saw declines on Tuesday, with the Dow Jones, S&P 500, and Nasdaq all falling due to First Republic Bank's significant loss in value. This drop offset predominantly positive earnings reports from the US. Treasury bond yields fell notably, and the Conference Board's consumer confidence index declined to 101.3 in April. While the majority of US earnings reports exceeded expectations, regional banks' deposit concerns continued to weigh on the market.

In Europe, most markets experienced losses after ECB Chief Economist Philip Lane stated that the central bank will need to raise interest rates at the next week's policy meeting. The Stoxx Europe 600 closed down, and London's FTSE 100 also lost value due to struggles in the commodity sector. In contrast, Premier Inn owner Whitbread gained after doubling its annual dividend and predicting strong demand during the King's coronation.

Asian stocks were broadly lower on Tuesday, with markets in Australia and New Zealand closed for ANZAC day. China's CSI 300 and Shanghai Composite experienced declines, as did Hong Kong's Hang Seng and South Korea's Kospi. However, Japan's Nikkei 225 saw a slight increase.

In currency news, the NZD/AUD rebounded to near 0.93 ahead of Australian inflation data. The Reserve Bank of New Zealand (RBNZ) proposed to loosen loan-to-value ratio (LVR) restrictions, while the Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA) anticipated a further slowdown in the Australian economy, affecting stubborn inflation. The cross experienced a significant sell-off in the previous two trading sessions due to expectations that the RBA would maintain steady interest rates next week.

Commodity-wise, WTI crude oil prices fell, while gold prices increased. NYMEX iron ore futures were last traded at US$116.76/MT. In New Zealand, Westland Dairy Company announced a NZ$70m investment in its Hokitika factory to boost lactoferrin production. Meanwhile, the Commerce Commission is assessing a complaint about the LPG market, and the Reserve Bank has decided to incentivize commercial bank involvement in the government's planned Business Growth Fund.

Today's events include the NZME and Summerset Group Annual General Meetings.
