Crash

Daily Report for Wednesday 12th April 2023

Posted by Ben Kelsey on April 12, 2023

The New Zealand Dollar (Kiwi) is under pressure and has fallen overnight across all currency pairs, with NZDEUR reaching a 3-year low. The Reserve Bank of New Zealand's (RBNZ) potential intention to push the economy into a recession could be a reason behind this decline. However, if RBNZ Governor Orr aims to combat inflation, a weaker NZD would actually contribute to increased imported inflation. The situation might be excessive, but exporters are taking advantage of it.

The US Dollar is weaker while equities are mostly up, although technology stocks are having a hard time. US Treasury yields are rising ahead of tonight's US Consumer Price Index (CPI) release, with the 2-year yield up 4 basis points to 4.05% and the 10-year yield up 2 basis points to 3.44%. The Dow Jones is up by 98 points, Nasdaq down by 52 points, NZX up by 4 points, and ASX up by 93 points. Crude oil (WTI) is trading at $81.37, carbon at $59.25, and Bitcoin at 30.1k.

Bitcoin has dropped below $30,000 for the first time since June 2022, but it's still 50% below its all-time high. The International Monetary Fund (IMF) has reduced its global growth forecasts by 0.1% to 2.8% for 2023 and 3% for 2024.

Fed's new voter Goolsbee suggests that the Federal Reserve should be cautious about raising interest rates too much, while Williams leans towards a more hawkish stance, stating that one more rate hike is a reasonable starting point.

China's credit growth is stronger than anticipated, with a 5,380 billion increase in March. Consumer inflation in the country has hit an 18-month low as factory gate prices declines have accelerated.

Australian housing prices might have reached a turning point, as they have increased by 2% since February. New Zealand usually lags behind Australia by 3-6 months. Meanwhile, New Zealand's QV housing prices have dropped by an average of 3.9% since the beginning of the year.

Warren Buffet is considering more investments in Japan. As for tonight, the US CPI is expected to be +0.4% month-over-month and +5.6% year-over-year.
