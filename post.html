Choppy

Daily Report for Monday 24th April 2023

Posted by Ben Kelsey on April 24, 2023

US equities finished slightly in the green after a choppy session, amid mixed economic data. European equities also closed positively, driven by strong earnings results, while Asian markets were mostly down, with iron ore futures dragging down Australian gains.

The New Zealand Dollar (NZD) weakened against the US Dollar (USD) on Friday, with the Kiwi dollar closing the week around 0.6160, down by over 1% for the week. NZD/AUD traded at 0.91752 on Friday, April 21st, increasing 0.00118 or 0.13% since the previous trading session.

New Zealand's inflation slowed more than expected in Q1, with annual headline inflation falling to 6.7% in the March quarter, compared to 7.2% in October-December. This development bolsters the view that global price pressures may have peaked, and central banks are nearing the end of their tightening cycles.

On the economic calendar, a quiet week ahead in New Zealand. On Wednesday, Statistics New Zealand will release monthly Trade figures, while the Reserve Bank of New Zealand will release monthly Credit Card Spending figures, an indicator of consumer spending and confidence. On Thursday, ANZ Business Confidence data will be released.

WTI crude edged up +0.1% to US$77.95/bbl, while gold gained +0.2% to US$1,994.10/oz. Iron ore fell -3.3% to US$116.00/MT. Bitcoin was priced at $27,574.

Seeka (SEK) dismisses the possibility of a capital raise, despite a difficult 2022 season for the kiwifruit sector with lower fruit yields. Synlait (SML) requested a trading halt on shares and quoted bonds, citing potential changes to profit guidance based on new information received. Australia's decision to ease citizenship requirements for Kiwis may exacerbate skills shortages in New Zealand, as more workers potentially leave the country.

Note: There will be no report tomorrow due to ANZAC day.
