Inflation Uncertainty

Daily Report for Thursday 13th April 2023

Posted by Ben Kelsey on April 13, 2023

The US Consumer Price Index (CPI) came in below expectations, increasing by 0.1% (5.0% annually), while Federal Reserve official Barkin suggested that peak inflation might be behind us. However, Barkin also mentioned that there is still a long way to go. The market is currently pricing in a roughly 18 basis point increase for the May FOMC meeting.

Equities attempted to rally, but the release of the FOMC minutes led most markets to drift off their daily highs. The minutes revealed that Federal Reserve staff projected a "mild recession" starting later in 2023.

US Treasury bonds initially rallied after the CPI release but reversed course later in the day, with the 2-year yield decreasing by 4 basis points to 3.98% and the 10-year yield decreasing by 1 basis point to 3.41%.

The US dollar fell following the CPI data, leading the New Zealand and Australian dollars to rise, although they couldn't hold on to all of their gains. The NZD seems to be oversold, and NZDAUD dipped below 0.93. Exporters are lining up at 0.9220. The Euro reached 1.1000, triggering several barriers.

Stock market indices saw mixed results, with the DOW down 38 points, Nasdaq down 102 points, NZX up 44 points, and ASX up 28 points. WTI oil traded at $83.32, carbon prices reached $59, and Bitcoin was at $29.8k.

The Bank of Canada (BoC) maintained interest rates at 4.5%, raising questions about whether the current policy is sufficiently restrictive. ECB official Holzmann suggested that the inflation outlook supports another 50 basis point rate hike in May.

Fed official Daly stated that there are good reasons to believe the economy will continue to slow without further policy adjustments. In New Zealand, ANZ increased floating rate mortgages by 40 basis points to 8.39%.

Dairy New Zealand reported a 1.26% decline in the number of cows to 4.84 million, with 20.78 billion liters of milk processed in 2021, a 4.3% decrease year over year. Today, the Australian labor market is expected to add 20k jobs, which would result in an unemployment rate of 3.6%.
