Daily Report for Monday 17th April 2023

Posted by Ben Kelsey on April 17, 2023

Just when it seemed like things were improving, we find ourselves back to square one. U.S. economic data has come in stronger than expected, beginning with retail sales figures which showed a smaller decline in core sales at -0.3% compared to the expected -0.6%. Industrial production increased by 0.4% month-over-month, and consumer sentiment rose by 1.5 points to 63.5, pushing inflation expectations up to 4.6% from 3.6%.

Fed Governor Waller commented that progress on inflation goals has been limited, suggesting that monetary policy will need to remain tight for a considerable time, longer than the market anticipates. In contrast, Fed President Bostic calls for one more rate hike followed by a pause, igniting a debate among policymakers.

U.S. Treasury yields have increased, with the 2-year yield up 13 basis points to 4.10% and the 10-year yield up 7 basis points to 3.51%. The market is now pricing in a 20 basis point hike for the upcoming FOMC May meeting. The U.S. dollar rallied, with the DXY index gaining ground and the New Zealand dollar being the worst performer, erasing all of Friday's gains. The NZD appears oversold, with exporters queuing up below 62 cents and importers staying above 63 cents.

The NZD/AUD currency pair is trading between 92 and 94 cents. The Dow Jones fell by 143 points, the Nasdaq dropped 43 points, the NZX declined 50 points, and the ASX rose 37 points. WTI crude oil is priced at $82.52 per barrel, carbon at $62.75, and Bitcoin at $30.2k.

Major banks reported solid earnings on Friday: JPMorgan's profits increased by 52%, Wells Fargo's by 32%, and Citigroup beat expectations. BlackRock CEO Larry Fink suggests that inflation is unlikely to fall below 4% anytime soon. In other news, China is reviewing tariffs on Australian barley.

Today, keep an eye on New Zealand rent and food price data, and on Thursday, the New Zealand CPI release. The market anticipates a 1.5% increase, while the RBNZ's forecast is slightly higher at 1.8%.
