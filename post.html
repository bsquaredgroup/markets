Low Low Low

Daily Report for Friday 21st April 2023

Posted by Ben Kelsey on April 21, 2023

Major indices in the northern hemisphere were down on Thursday due to downbeat results in both the US and Europe, with the Dow Jones and the Nasdaq both dipping -0.1%, and the S&P 500 falling -0.2%. European markets were also subdued, with the Stoxx 600 down -0.2% and London's FTSE 100 slightly up by +0.1%. Asian equities were mixed, with China's CSI 300 losing -0.3% and Japan's Nikkei 225 gaining +0.2%.

In the oceanic equiy markets the NZX -0.3% while the ASX flatlined finishing the day only marginally lower. The NZD/AUD currency pair traded between 91 and 92 cents, while WTI crude oil was priced at $77.82 per barrel, losing on the previous day. Carbon traded at $61.50, and Bitcoin at $27.2k. Gold and iron ore gained overnight.

US Treasury bond yields dropped, with the 2-year yield down -9bps to 4.18%, and the 10-year yield losing -6bps to 3.54%. Weekly jobless claims increased +5,000 to 245k, and existing home sales fell -2.4% m-o-m in March.

Notable stock movements included Tesla plunging -9.3% after reporting a steep drop in Q1 earnings amid disappointing profit margins, AT&T crashing -10.3% on weaker-than-expected revenues, and American Express losing -1.9% on missed earnings estimates. IBM gained +0.5% on better-than-expected Q1 earnings.

In New Zealand, company failures for the three months ended March reached their highest level since 2020, according to RITANZ data. Inflation in the three months to March was 6.7%, significantly lower than market and Reserve Bank predictions. Analysts questioned Michael Hill Jeweller's (MHJ) acquisition of Australian retailer Bevilles in a $45 million deal, with some doubting the ambitious growth targets. A San Francisco-based investment fund increased its holding in media company NZME (NZM) to just under 20%.

Enjoy the weekend!
